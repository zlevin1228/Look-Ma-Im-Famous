Legacy

George Halas (1895-1983), nicknamed "Papa Bear," was a pivotal figure in early NFL history. As a player, coach, and owner of the Chicago Bears, he led the team to multiple championships and played a crucial role in shaping the league's foundation. Inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 1963, Halas left a lasting legacy in American football and passed away on October 31, 1983.
